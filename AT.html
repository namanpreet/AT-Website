This Society is for the students who have programmers blood running in them. Our aim is to reach those who want to have a career in programming and android,web,unity or any technical field

Android Techies, in its endeavor to provide a platform for students to showcase their talents, strives for all to get inspired. It aims to nurture a competitive spirit in all, with the ultimate aim to learn and grow. The various new events introduced bring freshness and boosts the curiosity and talent of students. Comprising of immensely interesting competitions and informative workshops, the whole event brings a delight not only for techenthusiasts, but for students who wish to learn more.. This year, the event was aimed at greater heights and no stone was left unturned in making the event a great success.

Our Mentor

Gaurav Sandhu

Mr. Gaurav Sandhu has a varied experience of 12 years behind him. He received his M.Tech in ECE from Delhi College Of Engineering now DTU. Throughout his career, he has been actively involved in academics through teaching and guiding research students. He has been a very inspirational personality to students in technology. His research interests include Analog and Digital System. He has organised several free training programs for the betterment of engineering students. He has done a lot of work for students, most of which is practical. He has organised PCB designing Workshop in which students were taught to design PCBs including designing, etching, drilling and testing.

He has guided 22 B.Tech Major Projects of which two have won 1st Position in University level competition one has got 2nd Position in the same. Also, he has guided more than 200 hobby projects. He has assisted in placements of 432 students of GTBIT. He has also achieved a lot more things other than these. He has established Industry Institute Partnership Cell (IIPC) in GTBIT. He has done seven Industrial Projects, organised around 35 technical events during the annual fest of GTBIT since 2005. He has been heading TSG Society since 2005 with more than 1600 student members organising around 260 Technical events, 80 seminars, 62 guest lectures and 19 Industrial visits.
